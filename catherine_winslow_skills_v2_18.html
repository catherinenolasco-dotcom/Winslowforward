WEEK 1 · SESSION 2
Tech Stack Mapping: What you're running today
I map every system your HR function touches — and identify where Winslow connects, where it replaces, and where there are gaps to bridge.
  • HRIS + payroll: what system, how configured, how current is the data?
  • ATS: which one, how is it set up, is it actually being used the way it was intended?
  • Benefits: broker, portal, enrollment process — manual or automated?

WEEK 2
Pain Point Prioritization: What we build first
Not everything needs to be built at once. We triage together — by impact, by urgency, by compliance risk — and agree on the order.
  • Map every manual process: how long does it take, how often does it happen, what goes wrong?
  • Flag compliance exposure: anything with a legal deadline that's currently manual is Priority 1
  • Identify the "wow" moment: what's the one automation that would make your HR team's week immediately better?
  • Agree on Milestone 1 — the first workflow we're shipping, and when
  • Set expectations: what you need to provide, what I'll build, what Winslow will do

How we'll prioritize — the triage framework
Priority 1
Compliance exposure with a deadline
If something has a legal deadline and is currently manual — I-9 tracking, COBRA notices, final pay timing, required training — we fix this first. These are the things that bite you silently and show up as violations, not just inefficiencies.
Priority 1
Highest HR team time sink
Whatever your HR team is spending the most hours on manually, every week, without fail. Usually onboarding or answering the same 20 employee questions. Automating this is the fastest way to show ROI.
Priority 2
High-visibility employee experience gaps
Things employees notice immediately: inconsistent onboarding, benefits questions that go unanswered, no acknowledgment when they submit PTO. These affect trust and retention. Not urgent but high impact.
Priority 2
Manager-dependent processes that break at scale
Any workflow that relies on a manager remembering to do something — performance check-ins, onboarding tasks, headcount approvals — will fail consistently as the team grows. We get ahead of this before it becomes a cultural problem.
Priority 3
Reporting and analytics
Monthly headcount reports, attrition tracking, comp band gap analysis — important for leadership, but only valuable once the underlying data is clean and the core workflows are running. We build the foundation first.
